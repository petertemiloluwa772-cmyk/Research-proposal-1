Background to the Study

Defining Environmental Law

A broad field of treaties, statutes, and regulations designed to protect natural resources and human health. It acts as a "watchdog" for ecological well-being.

The Scope of Protection

Covers pollution control, resource conservation, sustainable development, and mandatory environmental impact assessments for industrial activities.

The "Why" Behind the Study

Human activities—industrialization, oil exploration, and improper waste disposal—have caused severe degradation needing robust legal mechanisms.

Rights and Duties

Creates a fundamental right to a healthy environment while imposing obligations on corporations and the state to prevent harm.

Historical Evolution of the Law

Colonial Period

(1900–1956)
Minimal focus, mostly public health laws.

Petroleum Era

(1957–1970s)
Sector-specific rules following oil discovery.

Rudimentary Stage

(1970s–1987)
Ad-hoc responses to environmental crises.

Contemporary Period

(1988–Present)
Post-Koko crisis: Serious institutionalization.

Core Enforcement Challenges

Despite robust laws, environmental problems like oil spills and gas flaring persist in Nigeria. The gap lies not in the absence of legislation, but in ineffective enforcement.

Regulatory agencies often suffer from:

  • Inadequate funding and technology
  • Political interference in inspections
  • Corruption leading to illegal permits

Structural Barriers to Justice

  • Economic Prioritization: Governments often favor oil revenue (95% of foreign exchange) over ecological safety.
  • Outdated Penalties: Fines for gas flaring are often cheaper than compliance for major corporations.
  • Litigation Delays: Cases like the Peremabiri spill take decades to navigate from High Court to Appeal.

Objectives of the Study

General Objective: To appraise the legal framework for environmental protection in Nigeria, examining current challenges and improvement prospects.

  • Analyze regulatory agencies (NESREA, NUPRC)
  • Identify enforcement bottlenecks and legal gaps
  • Evaluate effectiveness of the PIA 2021
  • Assess prospects for legal and policy reforms
  • Recommend strategies for compliance strengthening
  • Compare domestic laws with international NDCs

Core Research Questions

Institutional

What are the roles and interrelationships of regulatory agencies in environmental protection?

Challenges

What specific factors hinder the enforcement of existing environmental laws in Nigeria?

Prospects

What strategies can be recommended to ensure compliance and ecological sustainability?

Scope & Significance

Temporal & Geographic Scope: Covering Nigeria's legal trajectory from 1900 to 2026.

Significance:

  • Safeguarding human health and safety
  • Conserving biodiversity and habitats
  • Upholding "Polluter Pays" principle
  • Balancing economic growth with sustainable goals

Key Primary Legislation

Legislation / Act Year Primary Focus / Core Mandate
Constitution of the FRN (Sec 20) 1999 Fundamental duty of the state to protect environment.
EIA Act (Decree No. 86) 1992 Mandatory assessment for all major industrial projects.
NESREA Act 2007 Institutional regulatory and enforcement agency.
Climate Change Act 2021 Target for Net-Zero emission by 2050–2070.
Petroleum Industry Act (PIA) 2021 Regulatory framework for oil/gas environmental remediation.

Research Methodology

Doctrinal Approach

This study employs a qualitative, doctrinal method focusing on library-based analysis of legal materials.

  • Primary: 1999 Constitution, Statutes, Case Law.
  • Secondary: Law textbooks, peer-reviewed journals.
  • Tertiary: Law reports, reputable web resources.
